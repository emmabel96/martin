Paris - Summer 2015

I spent the Summer of 2015 in Paris, France where I learned about design and culture. This 5-week program immersed me in Parisian culture while I took courses in design principles by Miami University faculty. I covered several major topics during my time there: design principles relating to graphic design, music, and architecture.

Music

One of the projects I completed during this program was creating a soundscape based off a poem or work of writing. The goal of this soundscape was to capture Scott Fitzgerald’s interpretation of a summer storm. I successful created a piece that captures the playfulness yet daunting idea of a summer storm. Through this I mastered the art of using technology to create a cohesive work of music inpsired by creative writing.

Original Text: Summer Storm by F. Scott Fitzgerald (parts of text used)

Faint winds, and a song fading and leaves falling,
Faint winds, and far away a fading laughter . . .
And the rain and over the fields a voice calling . . .
Wait for the mists and for the blacker rain–
Heavier winds that stir the veil of fate,
Happier winds that pile her hair;
Tears from her wetted breast the splattered blouse
Of day, glides down the dreaming hills, tear-bright,
To cover with her hair the eerie green . . .
Love for the dusk . . . Love for the glistening after;
Quiet the trees to their last tops . . . serene . . .
Faint winds, and far away a fading laughter . . .

THEME BY ALLISON HOUSE
