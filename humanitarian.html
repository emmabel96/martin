Humanitarian Work

Inspired by social issues, I strive to make the world a better place for everyone. In doing so, I'm involved in many organizations and volunteer my time and talents to those who need them the most.

Peace and Justice Studies Association

Inspired by an outstanding social justice professor, I joined a small research team at Miami University where I helped with the maintainance of the PJSA website. The Peace and Justice Reasearch Association (PJSA) is an affiliate of the International Peace Research Association that brings together academics, teachers, and grassroots activists to explore alternatives to violence and share visions and strategies for peacebuilding, social justice, and social change.

Girls Who Code & ACM-W

"By 2020, there will be 1.4 million jobs available in computing related fields. US graduates are on track to fill 29% of those jobs. Women are on track to fill just 3%." -www.girlswhocode.com

This startling statistic poses a major social problem now and in the future. Women are already severly underepresented in the technology industry and organizations like Girls Who Code aim to solve this problem. Being a part of such an influential organization, I have had the opportunity to volunteer at Miami University's Girls Who Code club where I teach girls and boys from 5th-12th grade to code in an inclusive environment.

Inspired by the girls we work with and the club's amazing volunteers, a friend and I decided to follow this support through to the college level where we founded an ACM-W chapter at Miami University. The Association for Computing Machinery - Women's is a national organization that celebrates, informs, and supports women in computing fields. Being the President of this new club, I've helped to create an environment at Miami University for all genders and majors that support and further the development of women in computing.

Alpha Phi Omega
